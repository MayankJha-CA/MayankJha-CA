  • Modules Magazine (2014)

    Modules Magazine contributed some frivolous percentage to the "most startups fail" statistic. Modules was an upcoming magazine that aimed to promote budding musicians locally, by featuring them through articles and providing them the resources they needed to "make it big". Some of my peers in high-school started the project and I was naturally inclined to be a part of it in some marketing capacity. I ended up taking the role of marketing director, mostly responsible for promoting the venture and taking steps to fulfill its vision.

    Having never had any experience about marketing whatsover, I decided to Google how to look relevant. Eventually I came across some HubSpot Blog posts and faintly started to work my way from there. I came out with a bunch of strategies to monetize the magazine. A creative way of using inbound, I planned to publish paid articles written by local music schools and their teachers with a call to action in the form of their contact info at the end of the article. Another, campaign was printing the issues (which was an issue itself in the dying publication age) and getting schools state-wide to purchase them and stock them in their libraries. Using that, we were able to play with the numbers and use the outreach to have advertisers to use the paid space. Eventually, a lot of factors (lack of funds and hormones...) led to the demise of the "one-hit wonder".

    tl;dr I've briefly bulletted critical things I did below:

    → Boosted Facebook engagement to 4000+ members and 50+ active weekly users by creating an integrated social media strategy.

    → Developed an inbound marketing campaign for potential advertisers to generate revenue.

    → Generating outreach for sales through potential paid advertisers.
