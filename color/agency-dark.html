  • Modules Magazine (2014)

    Modules Magazine was an upcoming magazine that aimed to promote budding musicians locally, by featuring them through articles and providing them the resources they needed to "make it big". Some of my peers in high-school started the project and I was naturally inclined to be a part of it in some marketing capacity. I ended up taking the role of marketing director, mostly responsible for promoting the venture and taking steps to fulfill its vision.

    Having never had any experience about marketing whatsover, I decided to research digital marketing strategies. Eventually I came across some HubSpot Blog posts and faintly started to work my way from there. I came out with a few strategies to monetize the magazine. A creative way of using inbound, I planned to publish paid articles written by local music schools and their instructors with a CTA directed towards their contact info at the end of the article. Another campaign was distributing print copies of the magazine to schools state-wide to purchase them and stock them in their libraries. With that, we aimed to attract advertisers using our targeted reach. Eventually, a lot of factors led to the demise of the "one-hit wonder".

    tl;dr I've briefly bulletted critical things I did below:

    → Boosted Facebook engagement to 4000+ members and 50+ active weekly users by creating an integrated social media strategy.

    → Developed an inbound marketing campaign for potential advertisers to generate revenue.

    → Generating outreach for sales through potential paid advertisers.
